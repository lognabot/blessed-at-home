Understanding Your Options

Care Shouldn't Be Out of Reach

✝

Many families are surprised to learn how many ways exist to fund in-home care — from personal savings to veterans benefits, long-term care insurance to home equity. Our team can help navigate these together, removing barriers so care can begin when it's needed most.

Payment Methods

All the Ways We Can Help

✝
💳

Private Pay

Most Common

Many clients and families choose to pay from personal savings, investments, or assets. The most straightforward and flexible option — no insurance approvals required.

Liquidating assets a senior no longer uses — a secondary vehicle, vacation property, or other possessions — can also be a practical funding source.

Partner Tip: Private pay cases often have the fastest intake. Ideal for urgent placements that can't wait for benefit verification.
📄

Long-Term Care Insurance

Common

LTC insurance policies fund home care on a daily, weekly, or annual basis — often covering a significant portion or all of the cost. Policies vary widely in terms and benefit amounts.

We can help families verify and activate their coverage. Look for a history of premium payments to an insurance company as an indicator of an active policy.

Partner Tip: Clients discharged from SNFs or hospital often have active LTC policies they've forgotten about — it's always worth asking.
🎖

Veterans Aid & Attendance

VA Benefit

Veterans and surviving spouses who need regular assistance with daily living activities may qualify for the VA's Aid & Attendance benefit, in addition to their monthly pension.

Available to honorably discharged veterans and surviving spouses who meet eligibility criteria. We work closely with VSO partners and VA-referred clients.

Partner Tip: Many eligible veterans are unaware of this benefit. If a client is a veteran struggling to afford care, this is the first resource to explore.
🏠

Reverse Mortgage

A reverse mortgage allows homeowners aged 62+ to convert a portion of their home equity into tax-free income — without selling the home, giving up the title, or making monthly payments.

The loan is repaid when the borrower permanently leaves the home. For seniors who are asset-rich but cash-poor, this can be a powerful long-term funding tool.

Partner Tip: Best for clients who own their home with little or no remaining mortgage. Recommend speaking with an elder law attorney first.
🏦

Senior Living Loans

Specialized loans secured specifically for long-term care services — for up to three years. These are unsecured loans guaranteed by family members, with interest rates similar to a credit card.

This option can bridge funding gaps while families wait for other benefits to be approved or assets to be liquidated.

Partner Tip: A useful short-term bridge while waiting for LTC insurance approval or while a home is being sold.
Common Questions

Payment FAQs for Partners

✝

What if my patient doesn't have clear funding?

We can still help. Private pay is always available, and our team can work with families to explore reverse mortgage, senior loans, or VA benefits they may not be aware of. We prefer to talk through options rather than turn anyone away.

Does Blessed At Home accept Medicaid?

We are currently a private-pay and LTC insurance agency. For Medicaid-funded home care, we recommend the Florida Statewide Medicaid Managed Care Long-Term Care program. We're happy to provide referrals to appropriate agencies.

How quickly can care begin once funding is confirmed?

For private pay and LTC insurance clients, care can often begin within 24–48 hours of completing the initial assessment. VA benefit clients may require more processing time, but we can coordinate care in advance in urgent situations.

Can you help families understand their LTC policy benefits?

Yes. We're experienced in working with LTC insurance policies and can help families determine how to activate their benefits and what documentation is needed. Please include LTC policy information in the referral when possible.

Is Veterans Aid & Attendance available for surviving spouses?

Yes — surviving spouses of honorably discharged veterans who meet eligibility criteria may qualify for A&A benefits. This is often an overlooked resource, and our team is glad to help families explore it.
